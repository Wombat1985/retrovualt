Where to post first

Start with collector communities where feedback is welcome. Read the rules first, then post one useful message.

  • Facebook retro game collecting groups: ask for feedback from collectors and mention loose/CIB tracking.
  • Discord retro gaming servers: share the collector challenge and ask people to post their rank.
  • Reddit communities that allow projects or feedback: avoid link-dropping and answer every comment.
  • Product Hunt: launch it as a polished collection tracker once the site has a clean landing story.
  • Show HN: share the product story for a retro game collection tracker and ask for technical feedback.
  • YouTube and TikTok creators: offer a useful collector tool their audience can try, not a paid sponsorship pitch.
  • Retro forums and marketplace groups: post as a collector building something useful, not as an advertiser.

7-day launch sprint

  • Day 1: Post the Facebook feedback copy in one or two groups that allow app/tool discussion.
  • Day 2: Share the collector challenge in Discord and ask people to reply with their collector rank.
  • Day 3: Comment helpfully on five retro collecting posts, then share the tool only where it fits.
  • Day 4: Message 10 small retro creators with the creator DM and ask for feedback, not promotion.
  • Day 5: Post a short video showing loose vs CIB tracking and put the challenge link in the caption.
  • Day 6: Launch on Product Hunt or prepare the launch page and ask friends to comment honestly.
  • Day 7: Review admin analytics, double down on the source with the most signups, and write a new post from that angle.

Rules that protect the domain

  • Never paste the same message into lots of communities in one day.
  • Do not pretend to be a random fan. Say you built it and want collector feedback.
  • Make each post useful even if the reader never clicks the link.
  • Reply like a human, take criticism well, and ask what collectors want next.
  • If a community removes the post, do not repost it. Learn the rule and move on.
