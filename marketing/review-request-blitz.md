## Reply Templates

When someone says it looks good:

Thanks, that really helps. If you had to pick one missing feature that would make it more useful for your own collection, what would it be?

When someone says they already use a spreadsheet:

That makes sense. What is the one thing your spreadsheet does that a tracker absolutely needs to do before you would consider switching?

When someone reports a bug:

Thanks for catching that. What phone/browser were you using, and what did you tap right before it happened?

When someone asks if it costs money:

It is free to use. I am focusing on getting the collector experience right first.

When someone asks what consoles are supported:

It covers retro console libraries across Nintendo, Sega, PlayStation, Xbox, Atari, Neo Geo, TurboGrafx, 3DO, handhelds, and more. I am still refining the catalog, so missing console or game reports are genuinely useful.

## Do Not Do

- Do not buy traffic.
- Do not spam the same post everywhere.
- Do not post in communities that ban self-promotion.
- Do not argue with negative feedback.
- Do not promise features are already done unless they are live.
- Do not use AI-sounding marketing language.

## The Best Ask

The best ask is not "please use my site forever."

The best ask is:

"Could you try it on mobile and tell me one thing you would improve?"

That gets replies, and replies are what turn into traffic, backlinks, feature ideas, and trust.
